# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

CS students and classmates evaluating a peer's final homework project. Technically literate; comfortable with algorithmic concepts. Primary context: viewing a live demo or submitted link, likely on a laptop in an academic setting.

## Product Purpose

An interactive visualizer for graphs, hash maps, and BFS (Breadth-First Search) traversal. Exists to demonstrate understanding of data structures through clear, animated, step-by-step visual explanations. Success means a viewer can follow exactly what the algorithm is doing at each step.

## Brand Personality

Precise, diagrammatic, illuminating. Academic confidence without sterility. The tone of a well-typeset textbook brought to life — not a startup, not a game, not a toy. Quietly impressive.

## Anti-references

Nothing explicit stated. Implicitly: avoid the flat, clinical diagram style of VisuAlgo and Wikipedia. Avoid over-animated flashiness that distracts from the algorithm. Avoid generic SaaS dashboard patterns (cards, sidebars, stats grids).

## Design Principles

1. Clarity over decoration — every visual element must directly aid understanding of the structure or algorithm; remove anything that doesn't.
2. Academic confidence — typeset and laid out with the care of a serious textbook; correctness and legibility are the standard.
3. Interaction reveals structure — animations and interactions should explain what's happening algorithmically, not just look impressive.
4. Whitespace is teaching space — give structures and diagrams room to breathe; crowding obscures understanding.
5. Precision in labeling — use correct CS terminology consistently; labels and captions should be unambiguous.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Avoid conveying algorithmic state through color alone (use labels + shape + color together). Respect reduced-motion preferences for step animations.
